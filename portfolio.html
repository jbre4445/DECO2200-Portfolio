Autono-Move

Autono-Move is a app designed to help people with the process of moving house. It was designed with the intention of being used with autonomous technology as it becomes more available and accessible in the future. Autono-Move gives users the options to move belongings not just to new residences but to storage and other areas. By using the app, users can watch their belongings move without having to participate in the manual labour.

Check it Out

When using this app, users have the options of where to send their belongings, when they want to complete the process and the ability to track their goods all from the app. The user process consists of:

  • 1. Signing up and entering residence locations
  • 2. Selecting the services required
  • 3. Paying and selecting security measures
  • 4. Having a drone scan belongings to categorize ready for transport
  • 5. Booking transport
  • 6. Being kept up to date of the progress of the system as its transports the blongings

The "Autono-Move" Design Process

The design process was split into 5 stages each completed before revealing the final design. These were:

  • 1. Understanding the Project
  • 2. Intial Brainstorming and Research
  • 3. Establishing a Context Problem
  • 4. Concept Development (Iteration and Evaluation)
  • 5. High Fidelity Mockups

1. Understanding the Project

The first stage involved understanding the project and what it was asking from a design perspective. The aim of the project was to first either:

  • Identify a problem scenario where autonomous technology can be implemented
  • Identify a type of autonomous vehicle and find a area where this technology can be used

After narrowing down to a single problem or autonomous vehicle, the remaining stages of the project aimed to create a user interface for an autonomous system that would help solve the problem or use the selected autonomous technology.

2. Intial Brainstorming and Research

Through the second stage in the design process, the home was decided as an area to work with and design for based on problems that were found in research by answering a selection of questions:

  • 1. Where are stress points commonly located in the home environment?
  • 2. Are inefficiencies common to certain living situations?
  • 3. What are users current processes to overcome pain points? Are they useful?
  • 4. Is there situations where autonomous technology can be utilized in home life
  • 5. What are the current user attitudes towards autonomous technology advancements?

3. Establishing a Context Problem

By researching problems in the home, problem areas and user needs could be established to help direct the design process.

Problems areas

  • Organisation in the home
  • Busy schedules
  • Awareness of belongings
  • Chores and task requirements

User Needs

  • Privacy
  • Security
  • Help with home organisation
  • Relieving pain points in the home
User Storyboard
This was the point in the design process where a major change took place. By shifting the focus from inside the home to outside and more specifically moving home, the project brief would be answered better.

After this change a problem statement could be created that a design concept would have to answer.

  • Explore ideas for ways the moving house process (same city) could be more efficient, less taxing and more organised by streamlining the process with autonomous technology

4. Concept Development (Iteration and Evaluation)

This stage involved creating a developing ideas and iterating them based on feedback from user evaluation sessions. These ideas were created using paper prototypes. This cycle repeated in order to design a more polished concept.

5. High Fidelity Mockups

The final design stage involved turning the paper prototype into wireframes using sketch and InVision. The final design was created using Adobe XDCC. At each stage the prototype was tested and improved upon.

Working through this design process allowed for the creation of Autono-Move. Without proper technique and determination to work through each stage, the concept would not have been successful. Each stage tested the weaknesses in the concept to allow for improvment through iterations. There can always be improvements to the concept but the foundations as strong based on the previous work.
